About Us

ADI is a student group at Columbia University that aims to foster a community of students interested in computer science and all things technology.



Our Mission



Support

ADI welcomes students of all backgrounds, skills, and experiences. Newly intended CS majors/minors and current CS majors benefit from our various prorgams, weekly events, and mentorship opportunities that encourage skill-building.

Community

To create a close-knit community that allows like-minded students to connect, network, and grow their technological skills beyond the curricular experience.

Diversity & Inclusion

ADI is committed to promoting an inclusive and equitable tech community at Columbia by regularly hosting events like Cookies & Code. We strive to have inclusive and diverse initiatives to reach people who often face barriers in their pursuit of computer science.




So...What does ADI actually do?



Emphasis on learning the industy



We believe that industry experiences are completely different from what we learn inside the classroom. To get a better sense of what the tech industry is like, ADI hosts workshops, company tech talks, and plans visits to top tech offices.


Office tours during the 2022-2023 academic year included companies such as Bloomberg, mongoDB, Salesforce, and Google. Follow us to stay updated on upcoming events!

Education-oriented



ADI Labs is your opportunity to apply what you learn in class to real-life projects meant to help the Columbia community.
Through ADI Labs, you can gain experience working on projects either as Project Lead or as Engineer. As a member of ADI Labs, you'll learn to contribute to open source projects and to take your programming beyond problem sets.
Paired with one of our student mentors, you'll learn software engineering techniques used in industry. You'll also join an awesome community of builders that love to bring ideas to life and have a great time doing so.
